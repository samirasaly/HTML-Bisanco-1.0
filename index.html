Ingredients
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 pounds fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined skewers
Directions
  • In a large bowl, stir together the garlic, olive oil, tomato sauce, and red wine vinegar. Season with basil, salt, and cayenne pepper. Add shrimp to the bowl, and stir until evenly coated. Cover, and refrigerate for 30 minutes to 1 hour, stirring once or twice.
  • Preheat grill for medium heat. Thread shrimp onto skewers, piercing once near the tail and once near the head. Discard marinade.
  • Lightly oil grill grate. Cook shrimp on preheated grill for 2 to 3 minutes per side, or until opaque.

Ingredients
  • 4 Calamari steaks (1/2 Kg) (thaw per package instructions)
  • 1 cup Flour
  • Sea salt
  • Freshly cracked pepper
  • 4 Eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup Panko breadcrumbs
  • Canola oil for frying
  • Lemon wedges
Directions
  • Heat canola oil to 360° F
  • Slice calamari against the grain, into strips.
  • Dredge the strips in flour seasoned with salt and pepper, then the beaten egg, then in panko.
  • Fry for 20 seconds, until crispy and golden.
  • Drain on wire rack.
  • Garnish with lemon wedges and serve with your favorite cocktail sauce.

Miso-Honey Grilled Salmon & Sauteed Sesame Kale

    15 m

  • Total

    30 m

  • Serving

    4 m

Ingredients
For the miso-honey marinade:
  • ¼ cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • *sprinkle of pepper
For the fish and sesame kale:
  • 3 fillets sockeye salmon
  • 6 cups chopped kale
  • 3 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
  • *sprinkle of sesame seeds
Directions
  • In a small saucepan over medium heat, melt honey and olive oil. Whisk in miso paste and black pepper. Remove from heat and set aside. When ready to cook the fish, rinse fillets and pat dry with a paper towel. Add fillets to a small bowl and cover with the miso-honey marinade (reserving some marinade to add before serving if you wish). Heat a large skillet with a generous splash of olive or coconut oil, over medium-high to high heat. Grill fish for 4-5 minutes, or until the first side gets a great sear. Flip and cook for another 3-4 minutes. Set aside. In the same skillet, add chopped kale and cook for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened. Drizzle with toasted sesame oil, toss greens to coat, and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Optional to add extra miso-honey marinade to the fish before serving.

Fried Shrimp

  • Total

    30 m

  • Prep

    15 m

  • Cook

    15 m

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetuer adipiscing elit. Aenean commodo ligula eget dolor. Aenean massa. Cum sociis natoque penatibus et magnis dis parturient montes, nascetur ridiculus mus. Donec quam felis, ultricies nec

Ingredients
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 cup hot sauce
  • 2 cups self rising flour
  • 1/4 cup self rising cornmeal
  • 2 tablespoons coarse ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons salt
  • 2 lbs medium peeled and deveined with tails left on shrimp for frying peanut oil
Directions
  • Preheat oil to 375 °F.
  • Line a baking tray with paper towels and set aside. In a shallow baking dish, whisk together milk, buttermilk and hot sauce. In a separate shallow baking dish, whisk together flour, cornmeal, pepper and salt. Make sure your shrimp are dry and dredge in the dry mixture first, then wet mixture and finally the dry mixture again. Shake off excess between each dredging.
  • Deep-fry in batches, but do not overload the fryer. Fry for 2 minutes or until golden brown.
  • Remove from oil with a slotted spoon and drain on the paper towel lined baking tray. Serve warm.
